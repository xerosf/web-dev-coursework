Overview


What are Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are 17 global objectives adopted by the United Nations in 2015 to address various global challenges such as poverty, inequality, and environmental degradation. These goals would assist for a sustainable future by 2030.


The SDGs provide a universal call to action, urging governments, businesses, and individuals to work together toward a better world. They emphasize the need for economic growth, social inclusion, and environmental sustainability.

Climate Change


What is Climate Change?

Climate change is the long-term shift in temperature and weather patterns, accelerated by human activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation.


Without immediate action, rising global temperatures will continue to cause sea-level rise, extreme weather events, biodiversity loss, and resource scarcity.

SDG 13 - Climate Action

SDG 13 - Climate Action calls for urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.


The goal emphasizes the importance of integrating climate action into national policies, improving awareness, and fostering international cooperation to address climate challenges.


By 2030, SDG 13 seeks to protect the planet and ensure a sustainable future for all.

Why Climate Action is important?

Protects Biodiversity and EcoSystems

Protects biodiversity by preventing deforestation and habitat loss.

Prevents Global Warming

Reduces greenhouse gases to stop rising temperatures and extreme weather.

Reduces Extreme Weather Impacts

Lowers the risk of hurricanes, wildfires, and floods that harm communities.

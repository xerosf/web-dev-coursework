Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) - Overview

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are 17 global objectives adopted by the United Nations in 2015 to address various global challenges such as poverty, inequality, and environmental degradation. These goals would assist for a sustainable future by 2030.


The SDGs provide a universal call to action, urging governments, businesses, and individuals to work together toward a better world. They emphasize the need for economic growth, social inclusion, and environmental sustainability. Achieving these goals requires collective efforts, innovative solutions, and global partnerships to create a more equitable and resilient future for all.

About SDG 13 - Climate Action

SDG 13 - Climate Action calls for urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. It emphasizes the need for countries to integrate climate policies, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and strengthen resilience against environmental disasters.


Climate change is not just an environmental issue, it affects economies, public health, food security, and global stability. Without immediate action, rising global temperatures will continue to cause sea-level rise, extreme weather events, biodiversity loss, and resource scarcity, disproportionately affecting vulnerable communities.

Why Climate Change is important?

Protects Biodiversity and EcoSystems

Protects biodiversity by preventing deforestation and habitat loss.

Prevents Global Warming

Reduces greenhouse gases to stop rising temperatures and extreme weather.

Reduces Extreme Weather Impacts

Lowers the risk of hurricanes, wildfires, and floods that harm communities.
